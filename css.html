Hello Visitor,

Do you like to try foods from other cultures? There are delicious cusines in Saudi Arabia that would make your mouth start watering. The spices added into these meals add a lot of flavoring and most meals in Saudi Arabia are eaten with rice and sometimes with meat as well.

Al Kabsa

Hover over the blue graph paper to learn about this traditional dish!

AI Kabsa is one of the national dishes of Saudi Arabia! This dish is a well seasoned flavorful mixed rice dish with meat, vegetables and different spices making the taste powerful and an immaculate dish. This dish is normally made with different types of meat like, camel, fish, or lamb. AI Kabsa is a dish that is usually eaten for lunch or dinner and is shared with many people due the amount of servings it has for multiple people. The correct and polite way of eating this cuisine is by placing the rice on a large serving dish, topping the dish with the chicken & garnished with almonds or any type of nuts. Then, this dish is served with a fresh mixed salad.

Harees

Hover over the blue graph paper to learn about this traditional dish!

Harees is a traditional dish in Saudi Arabia! This dish is made with wheat grain, meat, and ghee. The way this delectable dish's process of being made is that the wheat would be soaked overnight, then slowly cooked with meat until it reaches a porridge-like consistency. You can use lamb or chicken as the meat for this dish. This traditional Saudi Arabian dish has a reputation of having a slightly sweet taste, but has no overpowering spices dominating over the taste.

Jareesh

Hover over the blue graph paper to learn about this traditional dish!

Jareesh is a traditional dish from the Arabian Peninsula, and it is a type of cracked wheat stew that is often compared to porridge. It stands as a prime example of Saudi Arabian cuisine's resourcefulness and ingenuity, using staple ingredients like wheat and meat. It underlines the significance of grains included in the diet of someone who lives in Saudi Arabia and diverse textures and flavors. To add on, the Jareesh is served tender, and the chicken broth is usually fully absorbed, stirred with ghee.

Ma'amoul

Hover over the blue graph paper to learn about this traditional dish!

Ma'amoul are a very famous kind of cookies in the Middle Eastern area, and usually are prepared in celebration of both Eid Al-Fiter and Eid Al-Adha. Ma'amoul are typically filled with different fillings and come with various shapes. They can be stuffed with date paste, ground flavored pistachios and ground walnuts that could be flavored with cinnamon. They can also be shaped using molds and they are usually circular.
